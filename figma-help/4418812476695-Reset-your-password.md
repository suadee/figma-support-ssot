## 내용

Who can use this feature

Available on [any team or plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328273)

If you log in to Figma using an email address and password, you can reset your password from the log in screen.

If you log in to Figma using Google SSO, your email address and password is managed by Google. You will need to [change your password in your Google account](https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/41078?co=GENIE.Platform%3DDesktop&hl=en).

If you log in to Figma with your company email address via SAML SSO, your organization's [identity provider](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040532333) manages your login details. We recommend contacting your IT department for help updating your email address or password.

**Note**: If you are already logged in to your Figma account and know your current password, you can update your password from your [account settings](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039820114-Manage-email-address-or-password).

## Reset a forgotten password

1. Go to [figma.com](http://figma.com/) or open the Figma desktop app.
2. Select **Log in** from the navigation menu.
3. Click **Reset password**.
4. Enter the **Email** for your account and click **Reset password**. If there is an account registered under that email address, Figma will send a password reset email to your address.
5. Open the password reset email and click **Reset your password**.
6. Enter your new password in the **Choose a new password** field and then **Confirm password**.
7. Click **Submit** to update your password.

Note: If you no longer have access to the email address you used to create your Figma account, contact [Figma Support](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041057214-Get-help-with-Figma#h_01J5V2MSWKPTK0Z9A4MCYBD1AM) for assistance.

If you’re not receiving our emails, a few things could be happening.

- **Check your email filters:** Sometimes email filters—either in your personal email settings or managed by your IT team—might block or filter certain domains or senders.
- **Check your spam:** We recommend checking your junk, spam, or other filtered folders.
- **Allow domain:** If you have IT support, ask them to ensure the Figma domain is allowed. If it’s your personal email, you can also add our domain or address to your safe sender list.
