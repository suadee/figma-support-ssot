## 내용

Who can use this feature

Anyone with verified education status can create free Education teams.

You need an existing Figma account before applying for education status.

## What is Figma for Education?

[Figma for Education](https://www.figma.com/education/) provides free Figma access to students and educators.

You can also follow the [Figma for Education](https://www.figma.com/@education) and [Figma Education Creators](https://www.figma.com/@k12education) community groups to access classroom-specific resources.

Note: In August 2025, we updated the verification process for Education teams. [Learn more about updates to the verification process](#updates).

## Feature availability

Figma for Education provides free access to one of two plans:

- High school students and educators get the Enterprise plan.
- Higher education students, higher education educators, and bootcamp participants get the Professional plan.

[See what’s included in the Professional and Enterprise plans](https://www.figma.com/pricing/).

Additionally, the following feature specifications apply to Education users:

- [AI tools](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/24039793359767-About-Figma-AI): AI tools are available for higher education and bootcamp Education users. AI tools are not available for K-12 Education users.
  - Some AI features may have limited availability for Education users. For example, [visual search](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/24037716110615-Find-assets-and-designs-using-AI) in the Figma editor is not available, but visual search for Community resources is available.
  - [The AI agent is not available while in beta](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/34932042346775-How-do-I-access-the-AI-agent-beta-in-Figma-Design).
  - [Figma Make](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31304412302231-Explore-Figma-Make) is available for higher education or bootcamp Education users. It is not available to K-12 users.
  - Education users receive AI credits each month. [Learn more about AI credits on the Education plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/37611757713431).
- [Figma Sites](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31230436657815-Explore-Figma-Sites) is available for higher education or bootcamp Education users. It is not available for K-12 users.
  - Each Education user can publish one Site file that they created.

## Qualifications

To qualify for Figma for Education, you must be:

- Old enough to consent (by yourself and not by a parent or guardian) to use the services
- A current student or educator in a classroom applying with their school email

If you are a staff member at a school in a non-teaching role, you do not qualify.

Note: Figma for Education is only available in select regions and may not be accessible everywhere.

### What qualifies as a classroom?

Students and educators in online or in-person classes and educational programs are eligible for the Education plan. Some examples are:

- High schools
- Universities, post-graduate institutions, trade schools, polytechnics, or colleges
- Select online courses, bootcamps, or workshops
  - Courses, bootcamps, or workshops should have structured, curriculum-based instruction with a teacher or mentor-led model. For more details on requirements, see [**Step 2: Verify your education status**](#verifyedu).
- Design and coding academies

### What doesn’t qualify as a classroom?

- Self-directed education
- Startups or edtech companies
- "Not-for-profit" or "non-profit" organizations
- Educational programs affiliated with organizations whose primary purpose is not educational

## Application process

### 1. Create a Figma account

All users must have an existing [Figma account](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039811114-Create-a-Figma-account) before applying for education status or before a bootcamp applies on your behalf. If you have a school-issued email address, please sign up for Figma using that email. If you already have a personal account with Figma, please [update the email](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039820114-Manage-email-address-or-password) to your school-issued account before applying.

Note: Your education status is tied to the email address you verify with. If you update the email address on your Figma account after verification, your education status will be revoked.

As part of the sign up process, you are prompted for the following:

- **What is your name**: This will be your display name in Figma
- **What brings you to Figma?**: Select **School**
- **What’s your role at school?:** Select **Student** or **Educator**
- **What kind of institution do you attend or work for?:** Select **High school, Higher Education** or **Bootcamp or online course**
- **Will anyone be joining you?:** Add students, teammates or skip
- **Have you used Figma products before?:** Select one
- **Which plan would you like?:** Click **Verify your status** under the Professional plan card

See the steps below for more details on the application process.

**Higher education applicants**

Figma partners with SheerID to verify all student and educator applications.

1. [Log in to Figma](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041064554) using the account you want to verify. If you already have a personal account with Figma, please [update the email](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039820114-Manage-email-address-or-password) to your school-issued account before applying. The domain of your email address must match the domain of your school institution.
2. Visit the Education status application page: <https://www.figma.com/education/apply>.
3. Select **Higher Ed** as your institution type.
4. Agree to the terms of service.
5. Click **Submit**.
6. Complete the SheerID form with your school and personal details.

**What if SheerID is unable to verify my application?**

SheerID will email you within a few days to confirm your eligibility. If they’re unable to verify you, contact the [SheerID support team](mailto:figmasupport@sheerid.com) for assistance.

**High school applicants**

1. [Log in to Figma](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041064554) using the account you want to verify. **You must login with a school-issued email address. Non-school emails will be auto-rejected.**
2. Visit the Education status application page: <https://www.figma.com/education/apply>.
3. Select **High school** as the type of institution you attend or work for.
4. Agree to the terms of service.
5. Select **Student**, **Educator**, or **Administrator**.
6. [If applicable] Select what subject you teach.
7. [If applicable] Tell us how you plan to use Figma in your classroom.
8. Confirm you are a current student or educator.
9. Click **Submit**.

**What if my application is rejected?**

- You must apply with your school email to be verified.
- If you applied with your school email but were still rejected, [please fill out this short form.](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeBVc7iw_JQzOsuL8GIu7bGZgaIkrFUIVTokQngaR5b-PQmHg/viewform?usp=sharing&ouid=109560398254177408179)
  - You were likely rejected because we don't have your school or district's domain in our system yet. Please tell us a bit more information and we will follow up shortly once we have added your school or district's domains.
- If you are a homeschooling educator or student, please [contact Figma Support](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/requests/new) with proof of your status to be manually verified.

**Bootcamp applicants**

Bootcamps will verify on behalf of their students and educators.

- **If you are a bootcamp student or educator,** there is no action needed from you. Please reach out to your bootcamp directly with any questions.
- **If you are a bootcamp admin,** you should [apply to join our list of trusted partners](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeZg_zGMmhuOqzLGHjuKDSdZSxh2w6SNBbEM6FbVCS9ioQj2g/viewform) and verify your students directly. Please read on for the bootcamp qualification requirements:

**Required qualifications for approval:**

1. Educational focus: Clear mission to teach design, tech, or creativity skills with Figma as a core part of the curriculum.
2. Structured course: Structured, curriculum-based instruction with a teacher or mentor-led model.
3. Legitimate classroom environment: Teaches genuine full- or part-time learners, not commercial teams seeking internal professional development.
4. Transparency: Public website showing curriculum, instructors, admissions, and tuition.
5. Non-commercial use: Figma is not used for client work, agency projects, or revenue generation.
6. Minimum student count: At least 10 students per cohort.

**Potential disqualifications:**

1. Self-directed, unstructured courses: Single-user courses with no teaching, mentorship or community components.
2. Independent tutoring: 1:1 or small group tutoring that is not part of a formalized program.
3. Trains commercial partners: Hosts one-off or continuous training for commercial clients seeking internal professional development, not full- or part-time learners.
4. Lack of authoritative proof: No evidence of bootcamp with a website or social media. Similarly, no curriculum, instructor info, or verifiable student activity.
5. For-profit client work: Bootcamps acting as design agencies or offering services to clients.
6. Misuse of EDU access: History of sharing accounts, using EDU licenses commercially, or reselling.

### 3. Create, upgrade, or join a team

Once your education status is verified, you can create or upgrade the team you plan to use as your education team.

**To create a new team:**

1. Open the Figma file browser and click **Create free team** in the sidebar or visit <https://www.figma.com/files/create-team>.
2. Give your team a name and click **Create team**. You can always change this after the team is created.
3. Start inviting students, educators and other collaborators to your team. Anyone you invite with **can edit** access to the team must [verify their education status](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041061214-Verify-education-status#h_01H2TXTF5CQZTM6BNKA1TVG9R9) within seven days.
4. Click **Continue** and then **Complete upgrade**.

**To upgrade an existing team:**

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com).
2. Next to the team name on the left sidebar, click the **Free** badge.
3. In the upgrade modal, select the **Education** plan option and complete the upgrade process.

**Note:** Everyone you invite with **can edit** access to the team must [verify their education status](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041061214-Verify-education-status#h_01H2TXTF5CQZTM6BNKA1TVG9R9) within seven days before they can contribute. **It is not possible to have a mix of free education members and paid non-education members on the same Professional team.**

### 4. Invite others to your team

You can invite students, educators, and other collaborators to your team at any time.

[Learn more about inviting others to join a team](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039481034-Invite-team-members).

If you know a person's email address, you can send them an email invite to join your team. If the person doesn't have a Figma account yet, they can [sign up](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039811114) when they accept the invite.

New team members have seven days to verify their email address and education status. After seven days, Figma will restrict their access to the team and they will need to manually verify their education status.

## Updates to verification process

In August 2025, we introduced a new verification process for all users on Figma’s Education plan. This updated process is outlined in detail in the [**Step 2: Verify your education status**](#verifyedu) section of this article.

All existing Education teams were required to complete reverification by September 29, 2025.

### Troubleshooting verification

**What if I didn’t reverify my education status by September 29, 2025?**

Following the September 29 deadline, the following changes will start to take effect:

**Education team members (not team owners):**

- You will be downgraded to view-only access on any education teams you were part of. You can still see files, but you cannot edit until you are reverified. You can still [move files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) to other teams you belong to. If you are no longer eligible for the Education plan, you can create a free Starter team or upgrade to a paid team plan.

**Education team admins (team owners):**

- If your team had 3 or fewer files: Your education team will be converted to a free Starter team. You can still access and edit your files, but Professional features are no longer available.
- If your team had 4 or more files: Your education team will be downgraded to a free Starter team and locked. When a team is locked, you can still download files or [move files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) to another team. Moving forward, you can choose to do one of the following:
  - Unlock your team and stay on the Starter plan: Reduce your file count to three or fewer by [moving files back to drafts](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) or [deleting files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047512294-Delete-and-restore-files). This will unlock the Starter team.
  - [Reverify your education status](#verifyedu) to unlock paid features again, at no cost
  - [Upgrade to a paid plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360046216313-Upgrade-or-downgrade-your-plan)

If you reverify after September 29:

- Once your reverification is approved, your education team regains access to Professional features, and you can edit files again.

**What can I do now if I lost access to my education team?**

- If you are still eligible, you can [reverify your education status](#verifyedu) to regain paid plan features.
- If you are no longer eligible, you can continue using the free Starter plan or [upgrade to a paid plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360046216313-Upgrade-or-downgrade-your-plan) to unlock Professional features again.

**My team was downgraded and locked. How can I unlock it?**

If your team had 4 or more files and you didn't reverify your education status by September 29, your education team will be downgraded to a free Starter team and locked. When a team is locked, you can still download files or [move files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) to another team. Moving forward, you can choose to do one of the following:

- Unlock your team and stay on the Starter plan: Reduce your file count to three or fewer by [moving files back to drafts](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) or [deleting files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047512294-Delete-and-restore-files). This will unlock the Starter team.
- [Reverify your education status](#verifyedu) to unlock paid features again, at no cost
- [Upgrade to a paid plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360046216313-Upgrade-or-downgrade-your-plan)

To move a file:

1. In the file browser, find the file you want to move.
2. Right click the file thumbnail and select **Move file…**
3. Select the **All** tab to see a list of all your teams.
4. Select a team, then select either that team’s **Drafts** space or a project.
5. Click **Move.**

**![Move a file from the file browser by right clicking.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/35273997268631)**

[Learn more about moving files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file).

**How do I reverify my education status?**

To verify your education status, review the [**Step 2: Verify your education status**](#verifyedu) section of this article.

**I’ve graduated/am no longer in education. What are my options?**

If you’re no longer eligible for the Education plan, you’ll lose access to paid plan features once your education status expires. Your existing Education team(s) will automatically downgrade to the free Starter plan.

- If your team has three or fewer files, you can continue using it with Starter plan limits.
- If your team has more than three files, it will become locked until you either:
  - [Upgrade the team to a paid Professional plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360046216313-Upgrade-or-downgrade-your-plan), or
  - Move or delete files to another location (for example, to your Drafts or another team) until your total file count is three or fewer. To reduce file count, consider trying one of the following:
    - [Move files directly in Figma](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360046216313-Upgrade-or-downgrade-your-plan)
    - [Delete files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047512294-Delete-and-restore-files)
    - [Save a local copy of your files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/8403626871063-Save-a-local-copy-of-files), then reupload them into a separate team. Once they’ve been uploaded to a different location, you can delete them from the old Education team.

If you want to keep collaborating with your classmates or colleagues after graduation, you can upgrade to the Professional plan at any time to restore full access to paid plan features.

**I am a student and have verified my education status. The team I am on is no longer verified by the team owner. What are my options?**

If your team owner hasn’t reverified their education status, the entire team will downgrade to the Starter plan—even if you have already reverified your own account.

- If the team has three or fewer files, you’ll still have access, but Professional plan features will be removed.
- If the team has more than three files, the team becomes locked, and all members—including verified ones—will lose edit access to files.

To fix this, you can do any of the following:

- Ask your team owner to reverify their education status to restore access to the Education team.
- If your owner or team is no longer active, you can move or duplicate files you own to your drafts or another team.
  - To move files, check out the [Move a file](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) article.
  - To download and reupload files, check out the [Save a local copy of files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/8403626871063-Save-a-local-copy-of-files) article.

This ensures your files remain editable and accessible, even if the original team stays locked.

**I verified the wrong account/email address and need to change it.**

If your Education status is currently applied to an account associated with your education email, Figma is unable to move your Education status to a different account. We recommend [transferring projects or a team](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407) to your education account instead.

If the Education status was applied incorrectly, please complete [this form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1nDXDJoM3tgB8HhH1opCYNm0QUXWhyNTEm3N99I35aUI/edit). It can take up to a week for the change to go into effect.

**I am in higher education and trying to verify with SheerID, but see a “Verification Limit Exceeded” error message.**

Once you’ve hit your verification limit, contact the [SheerID support team](mailto:figmasupport@sheerid.com) to reset your limit and continue working on your verification.

**I am in higher education and having other issues verifying my account with SheerID.**

If you are having issues verifying your account with Sheer ID, review the following:

- You must log in with a school-issued email address. Personal emails are not accepted.
  - If you created your Figma account with a personal email: update your account to your school-issued email in your [account settings](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039820114-Manage-email-address-or-password) before reapplying.
- Ensure you are not on a VPN.
- For document uploads, do not send course syllabuses. Your documents must prove your enrollment in the school.

If you are still having trouble verifying your account, work directly with the [SheerID support team](mailto:figmasupport@sheerid.com) rather than trying to resubmit multiple times.

**I am in higher education and have been rejected by SheerID.**

If SheerID rejects your application, start troubleshooting directly with the [SheerID support team](mailto:figmasupport@sheerid.com) rather than trying to resubmit multiple times.

**I am a high school student or educator and having issues verifying my account.**

If you are having issues verifying your account, review the following:

- You must log in with a school-issued email address. Personal emails are not accepted.
  - If you created your Figma account with a personal email: update your account to your school-issued email in your [account settings](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039820114-Manage-email-address-or-password) before reapplying.
- If your school’s domain isn’t recognized, [complete this form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeBVc7iw_JQzOsuL8GIu7bGZgaIkrFUIVTokQngaR5b-PQmHg/viewform?usp=sharing&ouid=109560398254177408179) for review.
- Homeschool educators or students: [Contact Figma Support](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/requests/new) with proof of your status for manual review.

**I am in a bootcamp or online course and having issues verifying my account.**

Bootcamp students and educators can’t self-verify. Your bootcamp admin must apply to join our list of approved partners and submit user lists for verification.

- If you are a student, reach out directly to your bootcamp admin for help.
- If you are a bootcamp admin, [apply to join our list of trusted partners](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSeZg_zGMmhuOqzLGHjuKDSdZSxh2w6SNBbEM6FbVCS9ioQj2g/viewform) and verify your students directly.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Figma integrated with Google Workspace for Education?**

Yes. Admins can manage and deploy Figma licenses from within the Google Admin Console using the App Licensing System. This takes about 5 minutes to set up once you [fill out this form.](https://www.figma.com/education/chromebooks/#apply-for-the-beta-today)

Figma also integrates with Google Meet so you can start a file from within a meeting. Plus you can attach Figma files to Calendar events using our [Chrome extension](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/figma/fkmaohpngenfoccdgceedjkfhkdcohmg?pli=1) and insert files into Docs and Sheets as [smartchips](https://support.google.com/docs/answer/10710316?hl=en).

**Is Figma integrated with Microsoft Education?**

Yes. Admins can manage and deploy Figma licenses with Microsoft Education. They will be able to use SAML SSO to automatically provision a licenses to each student and educator in their domain. You can get started by [filling out this form](https://www.figma.com/education/k-12/#contact).

**Why can’t I access AI tools, Figma Make, or Figma Sites?**

AI feature availability on Figma for Education depends on your account type.

If you are on an Education team and don’t have access to AI tools, Figma Make, or Figma Sites, it may be because of one of the following reasons:

- You are a K-12 user. K-12 students and educators do not have access to AI tools, Figma Make, or Figma Sites.
- You have not yet reverified your education status. In order to access Figma Make or Figma Sites, Education team members must complete the [updated verification process](#updates).

**Where can I learn more about how to use Figma products?**

Browse [Figma Learn](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us) to learn more about how to use Figma products.

For education-specific resources, we recommend:

- **Live training:** You can sign up for one of our bi-weekly [live training sessions](https://www.figma.com/education-livestreams/). We can also host a free training/PD session at your school anytime. [Submit a support request](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/requests/new) to schedule.
- **Video tutorials:** Get started on your own with our [YouTube playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLXDU_eVOJTx5XxU4ncwRmFKjXQhpRU0Cr) of tutorials.

**Are there free education templates available?**

Yes, there are hundreds of free templates to help you get started quickly. Check out [figma.com/@education](https://www.figma.com/@education) and [figma.com/@k12education](https://www.figma.com/@k12education) for classroom templates and lesson plans.

**I am out of AI credits. How can I get more?**

Education plan users get 3,000 AI credits per month. If you reach your monthly limit, you won’t be able to run additional AI actions until one of the following happen:

- Your credits reset on your next monthly reset date, or
- You move to a plan that gives you more options for additional credits

To learn more, see [AI credits on the Education plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/37611757713431).

**Who can I reach out to for help or questions with Figma for Education?**

If you or any of your education customers have questions, please [submit a Support request](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/requests/new).
