## 내용

We’ve made a few changes to how drafts work in Figma over the past few years. Here’s a brief overview:

- **Before October 15, 2024**: On the Starter and Professional plans, drafts were not associated with any specific team. You had a separate **Drafts** tab in the file browser that contained all your drafts, across all teams.
- **October 15, 2024 through October 9, 2025**: Drafts were moved into teams, and each team has their own **Drafts** space. Any drafts that were previously in the separate **Drafts** space were moved into a new temporary space called **Drafts to move**. From **Drafts to move**, you were prompted to move your drafts into an existing team’s draft space.
- **Starting October 9, 2025**: The **Drafts to move** tab will no longer be available. Any files still remaining there will be automatically moved into the **Drafts** section of a team on your account. Going forward, all drafts will live within a specific team.

![A graphic showing the progression of changes in the file browser for drafts. First, there was a separate Drafts tab outside of teams. Then, the Drafts tab was moved under each team name, with a Drafts to Move tab up top. Finally, the Drafts to Move tab was removed.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/34783230255895)

## What to expect

- You can continue working in drafts for free. Each team you’re a member of has its own **Drafts** section.
- Drafts in **Drafts to move** that you hadn’t moved manually will be relocated. Figma will automatically migrate them into the **Drafts** space of a team on your account.
  - If there isn't an appropriate team to move them into, Figma will create a new, free Starter team for your drafts.
- You still own and control these files. Drafts remain private to you and anyone you choose to share them with.
- Some collaborator permissions may change. In most cases, collaborators will be set to `can view`, but you can change editing permissions if needed.

## Frequently asked questions

Why is Figma making these changes?

We want to improve clarity and consistency across Figma. Previously, drafts existed outside of any team, which made ownership unclear and created friction when trying to collaborate or manage files. By associating drafts with specific teams, it’s easier to find, manage, and share your files securely.

Where can I find my drafts that were in Drafts to move?

If you didn’t move them manually before migration, Figma will move them for you. You can find them in the **Drafts** section of:

- Your existing Starter team — if you only own one Starter team at the time of migration.
- A new, free Starter team — created for you and marked with a “New” badge, if you had multiple teams or no teams.
- Your **Drafts** space in your organization — if you’re part of a domain-captured organization with external collaboration controls (ECC) or using [Figma for Government](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/22932461365015-Figma-for-Government).

If you're unsure where they went, check your **Recents** tab or use search.

I can't find my files in Drafts. Where are they?

If you previously deleted your **Drafts to move** files, those files were migrated to the **Trash** section of the new Starter team they landed in.

- Check the **Trash** section of your new Starter team to find them
- From **Trash**, you can choose to [restore or permanently delete files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360047512294-Delete-and-restore-files)

This update created a new Starter team for my drafts. What does this mean for me?

If you didn’t already have a team to house your drafts, we will create a free Starter team for you. This team functions just like any other Starter team. You can move your drafts into a project to collaborate or keep them in **Drafts** for personal work.

[Learn more about the Starter plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/13838684089751-Starter-plan-permissions).

How can I move drafts from a Starter team to a different team?

To move a draft from one of your teams to another:

1. In the file browser, find the file you want to move.
2. Right click the file thumbnail and select **Move file…**
3. Select the **All** tab to see a list of all your teams.
4. Select a team, then select either that team’s **Drafts** space or a project.
5. Click **Move.**

[Learn more about moving files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file).

How can I transfer a Starter team to an existing plan?

When you transfer a team, all its projects and files will move to the recipient’s organization.

When accepting the transfer, the recipient can choose whether to keep or remove access for everyone from the sending team or organization. The admin of the receiving plan must approve the transfer.

There are three steps to transfer a team:

1. [The recipient provides a URL for the destination organization](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA0WKJV21TRWXPE7VMC)
2. [The sender makes a transfer request](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA0Y8Y6Z5X82TFPGD0A)
3. [The recipient accepts the transfer request](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA07EAHF7F1ZWED8V17)

[Learn more about how to transfer a team](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407).

How can I transfer a project from a Starter plan to an existing paid plan?

You can choose to transfer the project from the new Starter team to another paid plan on your account.

When you transfer a project, all its files will move to the recipient’s team or organization.

When accepting the transfer, the admin of the receiving plan is required to accept the transfer. The recipient can choose whether to keep or remove access for everyone from the sending team or organization.

There are three steps to transfer a project:

1. [The recipient chooses a location for the project](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA09JQTGQQCDYDN9Q11)
2. [The sender makes a transfer request](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA0PJ290B0EWF27SJXW)
3. [The recipient accepts the transfer request](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA0BKBEHYD75A418FMB)

[Learn more about how to transfer a project](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/10250387712407-Transfer-a-project-or-team-to-a-different-account#h_01J5BDVCA03KDMPYKR88NK94ZY).

How can I download and import files between teams?

You can select which files you want to move between teams by downloading and importing them manually.

To save a local copy of a file:

1. Open the file you want to export.
2. Click  **Main menu**.
3. Go to **File** > **Save local copy** to download a copy of the file to [your downloads folder](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040521373).

To import a local copy of a file:

1. Open the Figma account you want to import the file to.
2. Drag the file into the file browser.

[Learn more about saving local copies of files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/8403626871063-Save-a-local-copy-of-files).

How can I switch between different teams?

To switch between different teams on your account:

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com/).
   - If you’re in a file, click the  Figma menu > **Back to files.**
2. Click the  dropdown menu next to your team name on the left sidebar to see a list of your teams.
3. Select a team.

[Learn more about using the file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser).

Where is the Drafts tab?

Each team has its own **Drafts** tab which holds all your draft files associated with that team.

To view your drafts:

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com/).
   - If you’re in a file, click the  Figma menu > **Back to files.**
2. Click the  dropdown menu next to your team name on the left sidebar to see a list of your teams.
3. Select a team.
4. Click the **Drafts** tab.

What’s the difference between drafts and files?

- Drafts are private to you and live in your team’s **Drafts** space. You can experiment, create, and iterate here.
  - Drafts on the Starter plan do not support collaboration with other editors, although they can be shared with unlimited viewers.
  - When ready, you can [move a draft to a project](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360038511573-Move-a-file) to collaborate with your team.
- Files live in your team’s projects and support real-time collaboration with unlimited editors.

[Learn more about files, drafts, and projects](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/1500005554982-Guide-to-files-and-projects).

One of my teams has a ‘Locked’ label. Why?

If a Starter team has the **Locked** label, it means the team has exceeded the limits of the plan. To understand why, navigate to the team with the label and click the banner at the top of the page. You’ll see advice for how to unlock the team.
