Before you start

Available on paid plans

On the Professional plan, [team admins](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/4420557724439) can purchase and manage paid AI credits.

On the Organization plan, [organization admins](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/4420557724439) can purchase and manage paid AI credits.

On the Enterprise plan, [organization admins](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/4420557724439) can purchase AI credits. Organization and billing group admins can manage access to paid AI credits.

AI credits are used to run AI actions in Figma. As an admin, you can monitor how your team uses AI credits, understand when people are hitting limits, and choose whether to purchase additional credits to prevent interruptions.

## How AI credits work

Figma AI uses a credit system that’s shared across AI features and products. Every seat includes a monthly allocation of AI credits, and the number of credits depends on your plan and seat type.

If your team’s usage exceeds the credits included with seats, admins can purchase additional credits at the plan level through an ongoing subscription and/or pay-as-you-go billing.

Note: Figma for Education and Figma for Government plans have additional plan-specific considerations for how AI credits are allocated and purchased.

- [AI credits on the Education plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/37611757713431)
- [How AI features work for Figma for Government](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/22932461365015-Figma-for-Government#h_01J0C1R46DM9N2CHD01GJFA76H)

[Learn more about AI credits and how they’re used across Figma AI features.](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/33459875669015)

## Seat credits

Every seat in Figma includes AI credits. These seat credits are assigned to individual users.

Seat credits:

- Belong to an individual user
- Reset monthly
- Don’t roll over
- Can’t be shared or transferred between users

Credits do not represent legal tender, currency, or stored value, and are not redeemable for monetary value. They can only be used for Figma AI features.

|  | Starter plan\* | Professional plan | Organization plan | Enterprise plan |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Full seats | 500 credits per month | 3,000 credits per month | 3,500 credits per month | 4,250 credits per month |
| Dev, Collab, and View\* seats | 500 credits per month | 500 credits per month | 500 credits per month |

*\*Starter plan users or users with a View seat also have a daily limit of 150 credits. If a user reaches a daily limit, they can continue using AI features the following day.*

## Purchase additional credits

Admins have two ways to purchase additional AI credits if your team exceeds the AI credits included in your plan.

- **Ongoing subscription**: Purchase a monthly package of credits for your plan. When a user reaches their seat credit limit, usage draws from the subscription credit pool.
- **Pay-as-you-go billing**: Only pay for credits as you use them, up to a monthly spending limit. Charges are added to your next invoice.

Admins can [manage access to paid credits for individual users](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/41944442159767), including setting custom limits or disabling access entirely. On the Enterprise plan, admins can [set aside and manage additional paid credits for billing groups](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/39663732696087).

You can use these options together, or choose only one:

- If you purchase the ongoing subscription, users can continue using AI after they hit their seat limit until the subscription credit pool runs out.
- If you enable pay-as-you-go billing only, users can continue using AI after they hit their seat limit. Any additional credits used will be billed on your next invoice.
- If you use both, subscription credits are used first. Pay-as-you-go kicks in only after the plan exhausts the subscription credit pool.

![A conceptual gif. Four users, represented by bars, fill up their seat credits. Three of them are given access to paid credits, so then those three use up the subscription credit bar, and then start filling up the paygo bar.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/38958831340567)

**Example**

Twigma purchases an ongoing subscription and enables pay-as-you-go billing.

- Jeanie, a designer, reaches her seat credit limit.
- Her usage first draws from Twigma’s subscription credit pool.
- Later in the month, the subscription credits run out.

At that point, Jeanie’s AI usage switches to pay-as-you-go billing, counting toward the admin-defined spending limit. Jeanie doesn’t need to take any action—this switch happens automatically.

### Ongoing subscription

Additional credits can be purchased through an ongoing subscription and are pooled at the plan level. Seat credits are always used first. If a user reaches their seat credit limit, they draw from subscription credits (if available and enabled for them).

Subscription credits reset monthly and don’t roll over to the next period. The price per credit decreases as more credits are purchased.

To purchase subscription credits:

1. From the file browser, select the  **Admin** tab.
2. In the **AI credits** section, click **View usage**.
3. Select **+ Add credits.**
4. In the modal, click **Add credits**.
5. Choose a monthly credit tier.
6. Review the purchase details, then click **Confirm** to complete checkout.

![From the AI dashboard, click the Add credits button to add more credits to your plan.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/38911294649495)

Ongoing subscription charges are added to your next Figma invoice.

To update your AI credits subscription tier:

1. From the file browser, select the  **Admin** tab.
2. In the **AI credits** section, click **Manage**.
3. Select **Manage credits**.
4. In the modal, click **Manage** on the **Monthly credits** section.
5. Update the number of credits you’d like on your subscription, and click **Review and confirm** to complete your purchase.

On Organization and Enterprise plans, you can only downgrade or cancel during the 30 day renewal window prior to your plan’s annual renewal date.

For more details on the AI subscription billing, select your plan below.

**Professional plan**

On the Professional plan, the AI credits subscription is a month-to-month
subscription, even if your plan is on annual billing.

- Charges are added to your next Figma monthly invoice.
- If you purchase the AI credit subscription partway through a billing
  cycle, charges are prorated for the remainder of the current month.
- The subscription automatically renews each month until you change
  or cancel it.

With a monthly subscription, you can make changes at any
time:

- Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated for the current
  month.
- Downgrades or cancellations take effect on your next monthly billing
  date.

**Organization and Enterprise plans**

On the Organization and Enterprise plans, the AI credits subscription
is an annual subscription that aligns with your existing Figma contract
term.

- If you purchase the AI credits subscription partway through your
  billing period, the cost is prorated for the remainder of your annual
  term.
- Charges are added to your next quarterly invoice.
- The AI credits subscription remains active for the rest of your current
  contract term.

You can:

- Upgrade at any time. Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated
  for the remainder of your billing cycle.
- Downgrade or cancel only during the 30-day renewal window prior to
  your plan’s renewal date.
  [Learn more about making adjustments at your plan renewal date.](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328293-Manage-billing-on-the-Organization-and-Enterprise-plans#adjust-seats-at-renewal)

Note: If your Organization plan is billed under
a custom order form contract, your AI credits subscription becomes
part of your contracted annual spend. This means you may only
be able to change your subscription by signing a new order form.
We’ll send reminder emails ahead of your renewal to help you
plan.

### Pay-as-you-go billing

Pay-as-you-go billing lets admins pay for additional AI credits used beyond available seat and subscription credits, up to a monthly spending limit. Pay-as-you-go credits are automatically charged to your next invoice at the pay-as-you-go rate in place at the time. The current rate is $0.03 per credit.

To enable pay-as-you-go billing:

1. From the file browser, select the  **Admin** tab.
2. In the **AI credits** section, click **View usage**.
3. Select **+ Add credits.**
4. In the modal, click the link to **pay as you go instead**.
5. Set a monthly spend limit.
6. Click **Review and confirm**.

Charges appear as pay-as-you-go usage on your next invoice.

You can enable, disable, or adjust your pay-as-you-go spending limit at any time. To adjust your pay-as-you-go billing settings:

1. From the file browser, select the  **Admin** tab.
2. In the **AI credits** section, click **Add credits**.
3. In the modal, click **Manage** on the **Pay as you go** section.
4. Adjust the slider to change your monthly maximum spend amount, or select **disable pay as you go** to disable it.
5. Click **Review and confirm**.

When you adjust your pay-as-you-go settings, the following applies:

- Changes to your monthly spending limit take effect immediately.
- If you lower your spending limit below the amount already accrued in the current month, additional pay-as-you-go usage will stop right away.
- Any pay-as-you-go credits already used during the current month will still be billed on your next invoice, even if you lower your spending limit or disable pay-as-you-go.

**Example**

Your monthly pay-as-you-go limit is set to $500.

Midway through the month, your team has already accrued $450 in pay-as-you-go usage. You decide to lower your limit to $300.

Because changes take effect immediately:

- Your plan won’t be able to accrue any additional pay-as-you-go charges for the rest of the month.
- The $450 already used will still appear on your next invoice.

### Manage paid credit access for individual users

Admins can control who is allowed to use paid credits (the ongoing subscription and/or pay-as-you-go billing, depending on what you’ve purchased for your plan). This can help you manage costs by limiting paid usage to specific people or teams.

Note: Figma is slowly rolling out the ability to set custom limit amounts per user. [Learn more about setting AI credit limits](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/41944442159767).

**Example**

Twigma allows subscription credits and pay-as-you-go for most of the team, but disables paid credit access for interns.

When Noah (an intern) reaches his seat credit limit, he’ll see an in-product message letting him know he’s out of credits. He won’t be able to use AI again until his seat credits reset, even though paid credits are still available for others.

To update paid credit access for an individual:

1. From the file browser, click the  **Admin** tab.
2. Select the  **People** tab.
3. Select a user.
4. In the **AI credit usage** section of the side panel, enable or disable the **Paid credit access enabled** toggle.

![Manage paid credit access from the user flyout on the People tab.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/38464293257879)

**Note**: You can also update an individual’s paid credit access from the AI credits dashboard, by hovering over a person’s row and selecting  **More > Remove access to paid credits**.

Billing group admins can enable or disable paid credit access for members of their billing group from their billing group dashboard using the same **Paid credit access enabled** toggle.

To update paid credit access in bulk:

1. From the file browser, click the  **Admin** tab.
2. Select the  **People** tab.
3. Select multiple people using the checkboxes.
4. Use the bulk actions menu to enable or disable **Paid credit access**.

**Tip**: Once you've purchased an AI credits subscription or enabled pay-as-you-go billing, use the **Paid AI credit access** column from the [**Admin > People** tab of your admin dashboard](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/4420842863255) to see which users have paid credit access at a glance.

## Respond to credit requests

When a user runs out of AI credits and can’t complete an AI action, they’ll see an in-product message. If your plan hasn’t purchased additional paid credits, users may also see an option to request more credits.

![Users can request more AI credits from their admin when they run out](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/38998277851415)

Requests don’t automatically purchase credits or change any billing settings.

When someone requests credits, admins are notified via email and via their  [file browser notifications](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039813234-Manage-your-notification-preferences).

As an admin, you can use this request as a signal to review usage and decide what action (if any) makes sense for your team. [Open the AI credits dashboard](#h_01KKHTG030H68474XVV7VTS85H) to review overall usage across your plan.

Depending on what you see, you may choose to:

- [Upgrade the user’s seat](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039960434-Manage-seats-in-Figma) (for example, from View, Collab, or Dev to Full) if they need a higher monthly credit allocation.
- Purchase an AI credits subscription if your team is consistently reaching seat limits.
- Enable pay-as-you-go billing to allow continued usage beyond seat and subscription credits.
- Wait until credits reset, if the usage appears temporary and you don’t want to expand paid access.

If you take action to add or increase paid credits, users will be unblocked automatically once credits are available.

### View historic usage

**Note**: Only admins on the Organization and Enterprise plan can view historic AI credit usage.

Admins can view AI credit usage from previous usage periods to understand trends or plan for future usage.

By default, the AI credits dashboard shows data for the current usage period. To view past usage periods:

1. In the **AI credits** dashboard, click the date range selector in the top right.
2. Select a previous usage period.

![Hover over the date selector on the AI credit dashboard to view historic data.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/38464288469143)

Admins can download a CSV of AI usage data by clicking  **Get CSV** in the top right.

**Note**: The date range you select in the dashboard only affects what’s shown on screen. CSV exports always include all available usage data and aren’t limited by the selected date range.

Historic usage data includes:

- **People who used AI credits:** The number of users who consumed AI credits during the selected usage period.
- **People at seat credit limit**: The number of users who reached their seat’s AI credit limit for the selected period.
- **Last activity:** The most recent time each person used an AI feature during that period.

**Note**: Historic data is available back to November 2025 and reflects data available at the time. Reports from older date ranges may not include all AI activity.

## Track usage with the AI Usage API

Using a plan access token with the Figma REST API, organizations on the Enterprise plan are able to get up to a year of AI usage data. The AI Usage API lets you fetch per-user, per-day AI credit usage for your organization's plan. Each row reports the seat-level and plan-level credits a user consumed on a given day, along with the container (workspace, team, and billing group) the usage was attributed to.

With the AI Usage API, you can:

- Track AI credit consumption across your organization over time.
- Attribute usage to individual users, workspaces, teams, and billing groups.
- Build internal reporting and chargeback workflows on top of AI credit usage.

For more details about the API and the supported endpoints, see the [Figma developer documentation](https://developers.figma.com/docs/rest-api/ai-usage/).

## Frequently asked questions

**In what order are credits used?**

Credits are consumed in this order:

1. Seat credits
2. Shared subscription credits (if purchased)
3. Pay-as-you-go credits (if enabled)

Once all available credits are exhausted, paid AI actions become unavailable
until credits reset or an admin adds more.

**What kind of usage reporting can I see?**

Reporting shows total credits consumed per user and across the plan. Admins on Organization and Enterprise plans can [view usage](#h_01KHQ02VXQ33AC77PP8G1MN1RW) by usage period and export historical usage via CSV.

Admins can also view [usage data for AI features in beta](#h_01KTVXP3411QQN0GQ7N7P89KHD) to understand current usage trends ahead of features moving into general availability.

Granular reporting by feature is not available at this time.

**Will credits purchased through the AI credits subscription roll over month to month?**

No. Credits purchased through the subscription reset at the end of each
monthly usage period and do not roll over.

**Can I decide who can use additional AI credits?**

Yes. All users have access to additional purchased AI credits by default,
but admins can turn off access for individual users.

**Will I be able to use both the AI credits subscription and pay-as-you-go billing?**

Yes. Credits purchased through the subscription will be used first, and
pay-as-you-go billing will apply up to your specified limit once the
shared credit pool is fully depleted.

**Can I enable pay-as-you-go billing without purchasing the AI credits subscription?**

Yes. You can enable pay-as-you-go billing whether or not you’ve purchased
the AI credits subscription.

**Can I purchase additional AI credits in the middle of my billing cycle?**

Yes, you can increase your AI credits subscription tier at any time.

For the ongoing AI credits subscription, keep the following in mind:

- Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated for the remainder
  of your billing cycle.
- On the Professional plan, the AI credits subscription is month-to-month.
  If you need to lower your subscription tier or cancel, changes take
  effect on your next monthly billing date.
- On the Organization and Enterprise plans, the AI credits subscription
  is annual and aligns with your existing Figma contract term. Downgrades
  or cancellations can only be made during the 30-day renewal window
  before your plan’s renewal date.
- Subscription credits reset each month and don’t roll over.

You can also enable pay-as-you-go billing at any time, or increase your
monthly spending limit.

**What are my options if my team or organization uses up all of the credits in our subscription?**

If you’ve used all of your available AI credits from the subscription,
you have a few options to continue using AI features:

- **Upgrade your AI credits subscription tier**: You can
  increase
  your available credits by upgrading to a higher subsctiption tier.
  - You’ll need to select
    a higher tier than your current one.
  - AI credits tiers are not one-time purchases and don’t stack.
    Upgrading moves you to a new tier rather than adding more
    credits to your existing tier.
  - [Learn more about how upgrading your tier impacts your billing](#h_01KHPZ56HS6QWA10X0YMTQ82BQ).
- **Use pay-as-you-go billing**: If your
  plan has pay-as-you-go enabled, you can continue using AI features
  and pay for additional credits as they’re used, without needing to
  make any changes to your subscription.
- **Wait for your credits to reset**: AI credits reset
  at the start of your next monthly usage period. If you don’t need
  more credits immediately, you can wait until your balance refreshes.
