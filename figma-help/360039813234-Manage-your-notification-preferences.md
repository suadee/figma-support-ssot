## 내용

Before you start

Who can use this feature

Supported on [any team or plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328273-Plans-and-teams-in-Figma).

Notifications help keep you in the loop with what’s happening in your Figma account.

Notifications appear in the file browser by default but you can also enable desktop and email notifications. Notifications from comments are grouped and sent every 30 minutes.

## File browser notifications

Figma displays any notifications next to the account menu on the left side of the navigation bar. If you have a new notification, Figma displays a red dot over the bell.

The notifications modal displays the 50 most recent notifications. From the notifications modal, you can:

- Accept or decline invitations to files, projects, or teams
- Click on a notification to open the relevant file or resource
- Click **Mark all as read** to clear any unread notifications
- Quickly reply in-line to single comment notifications

![Notification panel open, showing recent Figma comments and invitations with options to mark all as read, deny, or approve.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/25947395120791)

## Desktop notifications

Use the desktop app to view notifications from the Menu Bar on Mac or the System Tray on Windows. [**Learn more about the desktop app →**](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/5601429983767-Guide-to-the-Figma-desktop-app)

To access this feed, open the Figma desktop app and click. If you have a new notification, Figma displays a red dot next to the icon.

**Note**: If you’ve added [multiple Figma accounts](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041064554-Log-in-or-add-accounts), Figma only displays notifications from the account you’re currently on.

Desktop notifications are enabled by default. To disable desktop notifications, do one of the following:

- On Mac: Navigate to **Figma** > **Preferences** and deselect **Show Figma in the Menu Bar**
- On Windows: Navigate to the System Tray in the Task bar and deselect **Show Figma in the System Tray**

## Push notifications

Stay up-to-date with browser, desktop, and mobile push notifications, even when you’re working outside of Figma.

### From the browser

Get push notifications from your browser app for files you are not currently active in. You'll get push notifications for @mentions in comments, @mentions on a file, and replies to a comment thread that you are a part of.

To enable push notifications in the browser:

1. From the file browser, click the [icon] **Notifications** at the top and select [icon] **Settings**.
2. Click **Enable browser notifications**.
3. Your browser app will ask if you'd like to allow Figma to send you notifications. Click **Allow**.
4. Go into your device's notification settings and make sure notifications are enabled on your browser app.

To manage what types of push notifications you receive in the browser:

1. From the file browser, click the [icon] **Notifications** at the top and select [icon] **Settings**.
2. Use the checkboxes to configure your settings for file comments, invites, and requests.

### From the desktop app

To manage push notifications in the Figma desktop app:

1. Open the account menu in the top-left corner of the file browser.
2. Select **Settings**.
3. Select the **Notifications** tab.
4. Use the settings to configure your push notification preferences.

### From the mobile app

To manage push notifications in the Figma mobile app:

1. Tap your organization’s logo in the top-right corner.
2. Tap **Notifications**.
3. Use the settings to configure your push notification preferences.

## Email notifications

Email notifications are enabled by default. You can modify email notification settings for the following events:

### File comments

- All comments on your files and any threads you’re in
- Only mentions and replies
- None

Mentions include direct mentions of your name as well as mentions of any [user groups](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/38020006913943-Create-and-manage-user-groups) you’re a member of.

**Tip**: You can also turn email notifications on or off for individual files. [Learn more about comment notification settings](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360041547813-Adjust-comment-notification-settings?source=search) [→](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039825314)

### Invites and requests

- All types of invites and requests for projects, files, teams, upgrades, plugins, widgets, and branching and merge requests
- Only invites and requests that need a response
- None

### Community comments

If you have a Community profile, you can choose to receive email notifications for the following:

- All comments on your published resources
- Only mentions and replies
- None

  In addition, if you have published resources to the Community, you can choose to receive a weekly digest email that contains a summary of activity on your published resources.

**Note**: Disabling email notifications does not disable all emails from Figma. You will still receive important emails regarding account management, billing, and admin settings.

To manage email notifications:

1. From the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183), open the account menu in the top-left corner.
2. Select **Settings**.
3. Select the **Notifications** tab.
4. Use the settings to configure your email notification preferences.

**Note**: Disabling email notifications does not disable notifications in Figma. You will still receive notifications in the notification modal in Figma. It is not possible to disable these notifications.
