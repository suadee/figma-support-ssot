## 내용

Before you Start

Who can use this feature

Available on [all plans](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328273)

Anyone with `can edit` access can edit FigJam files

Anyone with `can view` access can leave comments in a Figma file

## Use activities to present files

**Google Meet** is a communication app allowing individuals and teams to connect, collaborate, and engage in real-time. Use Google Meet to conduct video conferences, share screens, and integrate seamlessly with other Google services.

You can use activities to present Figma Design files, prototypes, or FigJam boards, and interact with them without leaving Google Meet.

- **FigJam boards:** Collaborate and access all the functionality of FigJam boards. For those with edit access, you can explore, create, and edit FigJam boards as you normally would.
- **Figma Design** **files:** Present Figma Design files and prototypes without using screen-sharing. Prototypes are view and comment-only, even for people who have edit access to the file. You can click **Open in Figma** to open the file and edit from the web or desktop app.

**Note:** To allow others to see or interact with a file, you may need to adjust the [file's sharing and permissions.](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040531773-Share-files-and-prototypes) FigJam files also have the option to start an [open session](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/4410786053911-Invite-visitors-to-an-open-session) to temporarily invite anyone to edit the FigJam file, including those without Figma accounts.

### Share an existing file

1. Click **Activities** in the bottom-right of the screen.
2. Select **Figma** from your list of add-ons.
3. Click **Share existing file** and paste the link to the desired Figma Design or FigJam file into the text box. A tile preview of the file will appear.
4. Click **Start activity** in the bottom-right of the activities panel.
5. Click **Share this screen** to allow others to see the activity screen.
6. Click **Allow** in the pop-up modal to let Google Meet see the contents of the file.

### Create a FigJam board

Select this option to create a new FigJam file so teammates can brainstorm and collaborate together, even if they don't have a Figma account.

1. Click **Activities** in the bottom-right of the screen.
2. Select **Figma** under your list of add-ons.
3. Click **Create FigJam file.**
4. Name your file and click **Create FigJam file**. A tile preview of the file will appear.
5. Click **Start activity** in the bottom-right of the activities panel.
6. Click **Share this screen** to allow others to see the activity screen.
7. Click **Allow** in the pop-up modal to let Google Meet see the contents of the file.

## Use FigJam and Google meet on Google Hardware

You can launch FigJam both in and out of an active Google Meet call using Google hardware, including the [Series One Board 65](https://www.avocor.com/products/google-series-one-board-65/) and [Desk 27](https://www.avocor.com/products/google-series-one-desk-27/) devices. When the Figma add-on is installed for your organization, your members will automatically be able to access the app from Board 65 or Desk 27 devices.

[Learn how to create a FigJam board on Board 65 and Desk 27 devices →](https://support.google.com/meethardware/answer/14301849)
