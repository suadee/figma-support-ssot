## 내용

Who can use this feature

Supported on the [Organization and Enterprise plans](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328273).

Guests are external users with email addresses that don't match any of the organization's domains. They could be contractors, agencies, clients, or external collaborators. [**Members versus guests →**](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/4420557314967)

Admins can see which resources a guest has access to, as well as any recent activity. This includes any teams they're a member of, or specific files and projects they can access.

You can use this information to decide whether to downgrade their access, or remove them from the organization.

### Who can view guest access

Only admins can view a guest’s activity or access in the organization.

- **Organization plan:** only organization admins can manage seats. Team admins manage [team-level permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673).
- **Enterprise plan**: organization admins can manage seats for anyone in the organization. Workspace admins can manage seats for people in workspaces they manage. Team admins manage [team-level permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673).

## View guest activity and access

![The guest access modal, showing what files and permissions the guest has access to.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/28287138287895)

1. From the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183), select **Admin** in the sidebar.
2. Click the  **People** tab.
3. Select  **People table filters** to add a filter.
4. Use the **User type** filter to filter to **Guests** only.
5. Select  next to the guest you want to review.
6. Select **View guest access** from the options.
7. From the modal, you can view:
   - Their name, profile picture, and email address
   - A list of teams, projects, and files they can access and their permissions on those resources
8. Select the **Recent activity** tab at the top of the modal to view details of any upgrades and the last five actions they took.
