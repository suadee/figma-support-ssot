## 내용

## Move around

The hand tool allows you to move around within your FigJam file without accidentally
making selections, or moving objects.

1. Click
   in the toolbar, or use the `H` key to activate the hand tool.
2. Pan around the whiteboard using your mouse or trackpad:  
   **Mouse:**

   - Click and drag to pan
   - Scroll to pan up and down
   - Scroll + `Shift` to pan left and right
   - Go to
     > **Preferences** >
     **Right-click and drag to pan** to set your mouse
     to pan on right-click

   **Trackpad:**

   - Click and drag to pan
   - Use two fingers

Toggle the hand tool

Press and hold `Space` to temporarily toggle on the hand tool.

## Recenter

If you scroll far away from the starting point of your file, a **Recenter** button will appear above the bottom toolbar. Click **Recenter** to bring you back to the center of the FigJam board.

## Zoom in and out

When you first open a FigJam file, the default zoom level will be set to
**Zoom to fit**.

You may want to update this to a specific percentage, or use controls to zoom
in and out on the board. You can customize these preferences by clicking the
**Zoom/view options** dropdown in the top-right corner of your FigJam
file.

You have a number of zoom settings available:

- Use your trackpad or mouse to zoom in out on the whiteboard:

  - **Mouse:** Hold down `⌘ Command` (Mac) or
    `Ctrl` (Windows) and scroll the mouse wheel up to zoom in,
    or down to zoom out. You can also set your mouse scroll to zoom by
    default in the
    menu > **Preferences** > **Use scroll wheel zoom**.
  - **Trackpad:** Pinch two fingers together to zoom out
    or stretch two fingers apart to zoom in
- Use the field provided to enter a specific zoom percentage.
- Use the **Zoom in**

  and **Zoom out**

  buttons to adjust the zoom level at pre-determined increments.
- Use the **Zoom to fit** shortcut `Shift` -
  `1` to fit the entire working area of the board in the screen.

  Zoom to selection

  You can also **zoom to selection** by using
  `Shift` `2`
- Zoom to a specific percentage to display the board at the percentage
  of the virtual/actual pixel size.

  - Zoom to 50%
  - Zoom to 100%
  - Zoom to 200%
