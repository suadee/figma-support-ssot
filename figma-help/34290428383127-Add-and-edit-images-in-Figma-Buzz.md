In Figma Buzz, you can add images directly to your asset from multiple sources:

- Browse free stock photos and illustrations from the built-in Unsplash library
- Upload files from your computer
- Generate new images using AI tools

**Tip**: Want to add a video? See [Use videos in Figma Buzz](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/33799379167767-Use-videos-in-Figma-Buzz).

## Add new images

If you’re creating an asset from scratch, you can insert images from stock, upload your own, or generate them with AI.

![Add an image to Figma Buzz.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/34291095246615)

**Note**:If you’re [using a template with guidelines](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31271733056023-Use-templates-with-guidelines-in-Figma-Buzz), you can’t add new images directly. Instead, you can replace existing images if the template allows it.

### Browse stock photos

Search a built-in library of free, high-quality photos and illustrations from Unsplash without leaving your file.

**Note:** Before publishing, please make sure you have the rights to all content in your asset and are following Figma's Acceptable Publication Policy. Please note that images provided by Figma Buzz come from Unsplash. If you plan to use any images from Figma Buzz, you are responsible for vetting them for your particular use case. They are subject to Unsplash Terms and Conditions available at <https://unsplash.com/terms>.

To add a new stock image to your asset:

1. In the left sidebar, click  **Media**.
2. Use the search bar to find the image you want.
3. (Optional) Click  **More details** to view image information.
4. Click the image to add it to your asset. If you selected a shape, the image will be applied as a fill.

### Upload your own images

If you have brand assets, product shots, or any other custom visuals, you can upload them directly from your computer.

1. In the left sidebar, click  **Media**.
2. Select **Upload image or video**.
3. Select a file from your device.

### Generate an image with AI

Use AI to create custom images from a text prompt:

1. In the left sidebar, click  **Media**.
2. Select  **Make an image**.
3. Enter a descriptive prompt.
4. From the dropdown menu, select an available AI model.
5. Click **Make it.**

[Learn more about AI image tools in Figma.](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/24004542669463-Make-or-edit-an-image-with-AI)

Note: If you don’t see an option to create AI images, you may not have access to AI tools in Figma. [Learn more about AI access](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/24039793359767-About-Figma-AI#h_01JT3QHH7EGEVMZQD8F405PJ49).

## Replace an existing image

You can replace any image with a stock photo, uploaded file, or AI-generated image.

1. Select the image or node.
2. In the inline toolbar, click **Fill > Image & video**.
3. Select **Choose media**.
4. Upload your own, browse the stock library, or generate an image.

![Replace an image in Figma Buzz.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/34291171431575)

If you’re [using a template with guidelines](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31271733056023-Use-templates-with-guidelines-in-Figma-Buzz), you can replace images from the **Edit content** tab.

1. Select the asset.
2. Click  **Edit content** on the left navigation bar.
3. Hover over the image and select **Choose media**.
4. Upload your own, browse the stock library, or generate an image.

If an image isn’t replaceable from the **Edit content** tab, [the template publisher has locked it](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31271693614487-Create-and-publish-Figma-Buzz-templates). You can only replace locked images if you remove template guidelines.

## Edit an image

Once you’ve added an image to your asset, you can make some adjustments.

1. Select the image.
2. From the inline toolbar, you can:
   - **Fill** >  **Adjust image:** Change exposure, contrast, saturation, temperature, tint, highlights, or shadows
   - **Fill** >  **AI tools:** [Remove background, boost resolution, or edit the image with a prompt](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/24004542669463-Make-or-edit-an-image-with-AI)
   - **Border:** Add a border stroke
   - **Crop:** Adjust crop or fill, flip, or rotate
   - **Corners:** Round image corners
   - **Effects:** Adjust the image’s shadow, opacity, blur, or overlay

## Admin image controls

On the Enterprise plan, organization admins can control access to the Unsplash stock image library.

To disable Unsplash images from Figma Buzz:

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com/).
   - If you’re inside a file, click the  Figma menu > **Back to files.**
2. Select  **Admin**.
3. Select  **Settings**.
4. Under **Resources,** toggle **Unsplash images** on or off.

**Note**: Want to disable AI images? See [Manage AI settings and content training](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/17725942479127-Manage-AI-settings-and-content-training-for-your-team-or-organization).
