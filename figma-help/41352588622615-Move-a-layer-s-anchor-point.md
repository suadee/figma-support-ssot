Before you Start

Who can use this feature

Available on [any plan](https://figma.zendesk.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328273-Choose-a-Figma-Plan)

Anyone with `can edit` access to a file can move a layer's anchor point

Rotation and scale transformations occur around a object’s anchor point. By default, the anchor point is located at the center of the object.

There may be times when you want a transformation to begin from a different origin point. You can use **Edit anchor point** to move the anchor point to another location within the object.

![](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/41352788137879)

**Note:** An object’s X and Y position may differ between Design mode and Motion mode. In Design mode, position is always measured from the object’s upper-left corner. In Motion mode, position is measured from the object’s center by default. If you move the object's anchor point, position will be measured from the new location instead. This prevents position and rotation animations from interfering with each other.

## Move a layer’s anchor point

1. Use the toolbar toggle to switch to  **Motion**.
2. Select the layer you want to update.
3. Click **Edit anchor point** in the **Transform** section of the right sidebar or use the shortcut to reveal the anchor point target:
   - **Mac:** `Option` `R`
   - **Windows:** `Alt` `R`
4. Click and drag the target to move the anchor point.
