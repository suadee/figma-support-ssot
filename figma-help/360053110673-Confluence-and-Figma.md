## 내용

Confluence is a team workspace where knowledge and collaboration meet. Equipped with the right information, teams can make quick decisions, gain alignment, and transform the way they work together.

Create a Confluence account and learn more at [atlassian.com](https://www.atlassian.com/software/confluence).

## Embed a Figma file or prototype in Confluence

[Figma Live Embeds](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039827134-Figma-Live-Embed) allow you to add live Figma files or prototypes into other websites or apps. Add Figma links to your Confluence Pages documents to:

- Embed Figma files inside your meeting notes or design specs
- Share your designs with teammates
- Collect design feedback from collaborators

You can embed any file or prototype, regardless of the file's [link sharing settings](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040531773).

Collaborators can interact with Figma Live Embeds based on:

- Their [team](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673) or [Organization permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039960434)
- The file's [link sharing permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040531773-Share-Files-with-anyone-using-Link-Sharing#Update_Link_Sharing)

### In Figma

1. Open the file you want to embed in your Confluence doc.
2. To link to a specific frame in the Figma file, select the frame.
3. Click the **Share** button in the toolbar.
4. Update your [link sharing permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040531773-Share-Files-with-anyone-using-Link-Sharing#Update_Link_Sharing) to determine who can view and interact with the file embed (Optional).
5. Click **Copy link** when you're ready to embed the file.

Note: Collaborators need to be signed in to Figma to [view and interact with embedded files](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360051741274#Authentication).

### In Confluence

1. Open the Confluence page you want to embed the file or prototype in.
2. Paste the copied link in your page. Connect your Figma and Confluence accounts if needed.
3. By default, Confluence posts external links inline within your page. Click the link to use the dropdown menu to display your link as a card or embed.  
   ![Dropdown menu in Confluence for choosing display options: inline, card, or embed. "Display card" is highlighted.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/360085937133)
4. Select **Display card** to provide your team with information on the file including an image thumbnail, comments, time last updated, and a preview.  
   ![Dropdown menu in Confluence with options to display a Figma link as inline, card, or embed, highlighting "Display embed."](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/360084742514)
5. Select **Display embed** to [interact with the file or prototype](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360051741274-Interact-with-embeds) from your Confluence page.  
   ![Confluence page showing embedded Figma file with dropdown for display options: inline, card, or embed. "Display card" is highlighted.](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/360085939073)

Tip: Learn more about using [links in Confluence](https://support.atlassian.com/confluence-cloud/docs/insert-links-and-anchors/).

## Use the Confluence for Figma widget

The Confluence for Figma widget allows you to embed Confluence pages in your Figma files, keeping project information and documentation next to designs, reducing context switching.

The Confluence for Figma widget enables you to:

- View project details in the embed card that auto sync with real-time changes in Confluence
- Paste a Confluence URL inside Figma to quickly access project information

[Get started with the Confluence for Figma widget in the Figma Community.](https://www.figma.com/community/widget/1420720697481932939)
