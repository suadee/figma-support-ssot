## 내용

Before you start

Who can use this feature

Figma Make is available for [Full seats](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039960434) on paid plans.

You can try Figma Make on [other seats and plans](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31722591905559-Figma-Make-FAQs#h_01JZQX7SQ7RX4SJ65DHDTYS2EK).

To get people viewing, using, and remixing your functional prototype or web app, you can share your functional prototype or web app to the Figma Community. Publishing to the Community from Figma Make only takes a few quick steps.

**Important:** Currently, conversation history is displayed when someone [remixes](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/33681473825303) your functional prototype or web app. Coming soon, you’ll be able to choose to hide the conversation history.

![](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/33652234861335)

After it’s published, you can make changes to the Community page, such as changing the screenshot, adding details about your functional prototype or web app, and more.

When you publish a functional prototype or web app to the Community, Figma Make also publishes to the public web so your functional prototype or web app can be accessed from the corresponding Community page.

**Note:** Full seats on paid plans can also [publish to the public web](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/31304586129559-Publish-update-or-unpublish-a-functional-prototype-or-web-app) without publishing to the Figma Community.

If you’re on a Starter plan, you’ll need to publish to the Figma Community in order to publish to the public web.

Dev, Collab, and View seats on paid plans cannot publish to the Figma Community or the public web.

## Publish to the Community

To publish your functional prototypes or web apps to the Figma Community, you must have a Full seat on a paid plan or a Starter plan, and `can edit` access to the Figma Make file you’re publishing from.

[](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/33652216658839)

To publish a functional prototype or web app:

1. Go the Figma Make file that has the functional prototype or web app that you want to publish. You must be the owner of the file or have `can edit` access.
2. In the upper-right corner, click **Publish**.
3. Toggle **Feature on Community** on.
4. Click **Publish**.

When your functional prototype or web app is successfully published to the Community, a preview of the Community page appears. You can return to your Figma Make file, or go to Community page and make changes to it.

## Edit the Community page

After you publish the Community page for your functional prototype or web app, you can make changes to the Community page.

To edit the Community page:

1. Go to the Community page for the functional prototype or web app.
2. In the upper-right corner, click **Manage resources**, then **Edit this page**.
3. In the **Publish your file to the Community** modal, you can change the name of your Community page. Optionally, you can also add a description, and tags to help people discover the Community page. Then, click **Next**.
4. On the **Thumbnail preview** step, click **Next**.
5. On the **Add the final details** step, optionally, you can add any additional contributors that you want to attribute, and a support contact. You can also toggle whether people can leave comments on the Community page. Then, click **Publish**.

## Unpublish the Community page

After you publish the Community page for your functional prototype or web app, you can unpublish the page at any time.

To unpublish the Community page for your functional prototype or web app:

1. Go to the Community page for the functional prototype or web app.
2. In the upper-right corner, click **Manage resources**, then **Unpublish**.
3. In the **Remove file from Community hub** modal, click **Unpublish**.

**Important:** Unpublishing your Community page also unpublishes your functional prototype or web app from the public web. This happens even if you’d previously published to the public web before publishing to the Community.
