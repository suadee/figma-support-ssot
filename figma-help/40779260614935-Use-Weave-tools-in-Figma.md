Weave tools in Figma are in open beta. They are free to use during the beta, so AI credits are not consumed for any prompts or actions. This allows you to explore and experiment without worrying about credit usage. Once Weave tools in Figma become generally available, AI credit usage will apply.

Weave tools are AI-powered apps you can use on your design content. For example, you can use Weave tools to generate variations, retouch an image, or apply a style.

To get started, select an image on your Figma Design canvas and choose a tool, or browse available tools first and select your input after.

**Note**: In October 2025, Figma announced the acquisition of Weavy, now Figma Weave. Figma Weave is a creative platform for AI-native media generation and editing. Figma Weave is available today as a standalone experience at [weave.figma.com](https://weave.figma.com/). Over time, Figma Weave will integrate into the rest of the Figma platform.

[Learn more about Figma Weave](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/35965787376919-Figma-Weave-FAQ).

## Use a Weave tool on an image

To use a Weave tool on an image:

1. Select  **Tools** from the navigation bar.
2. Click the  **Filter** icon and select the **Weave** resource type.
3. Browse the available Weave tools and select the one you want to use.
4. In the modal, configure the settings for that tool. Depending on the tool, this may include selecting or uploading a reference image, entering a text prompt, or choosing options from a dropdown.
5. Select how many runs you want to generate, then click **Generate**.

![Select the tools icon from the left sidebar to search for weavy tools](https://help.figma.com/hc/article_attachments/41648211793559)

Some examples of Weave tools you can use in Figma include:

- **Generate mockup:** Visualize your design in a physical placement, like an out-of-home campaign or product packaging.
- **Transfer style:** Apply the visual style of a source image to a target image, making them look consistent.
- **Replace background:** Describe a new background and replace the existing one in your image.
- **Change lighting:** Choose a lighting option and apply it to an image.
- **Text to vector illustration:** Turn a text prompt into a minimalist vector illustration.
- **Apply color palette:** Take the color palette from one image and apply it to another.

**Note**: The Weave tools available in Figma are curated by Figma. To see more Weave workflows that you can explore further in Figma Weave, visit the [Figma Community](https://www.figma.com/community/weave).

## Open a workflow in Weave

Each Weave tool in Figma is powered by a node-based workflow built in Figma Weave. You can open any tool's underlying workflow in Figma Weave to explore how it works, duplicate it, and build on top of it.

Figma and Figma Weave are currently separate products. To use Figma Weave, you'll need a Figma Weave account—but if you already have a Figma account, you can sign in at [weave.figma.com](https://weave.figma.com/) using your existing Figma credentials.

To open a workflow in Weave:

1. Select  **Tools** from the navigation bar.
2. Click the  **Filter** icon and select the **Weave** resource type.
3. Hover over a Weave tool and click  the three dots > **Open in Weave**.

[Learn more about working in Figma Weave](https://help.weavy.ai/en/).

**Note**: Actions in Figma Weave consume Figma Weave AI credits. [Learn more about Figma Weave AI credits](https://help.weavy.ai/en/articles/12267166-weavy-s-credit-system).
