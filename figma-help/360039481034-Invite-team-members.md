## 내용

Before you start

Who can use this feature

Available on [any plan](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360040328273).

Teams are designated spaces for projects and files. Use teams to collaborate with others, or to organize your work.

Any team member can invite collaborators to a team.

You can only invite someone at a permission level equal to or lower than your own:

- If you have `can edit` team permissions → Can invite others with `can edit` or `can view` team permissions
- If you have `can view` team permissions → Can invite others with `can view` team permissions only

Tip: Learn more about [teams in Figma](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039480614) and [team permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673).

## Invite by email

If you know the person’s email address, you can send a direct invite.

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com/).
   - If you’re in a Figma file, click the  Figma menu > **Back to files.**
2. Navigate to the team page.
   - On the Starter or Professional plan, select  **All projects** from the left sidebar.
   - On the Organization or Enterprise plan without workspaces enabled, select  **All teams** from the left sidebar. Then, select a team.
   - On the Enterprise plan with workspaces enabled, select  **All workspaces** from the left sidebar. Then, select a team from the **Your teams** tab.
3. Click **Share**.
4. Enter the person’s email address and set their [team permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673).
5. Click **Invite**.

They’ll get an email and an in-app notification (if they already have a Figma account) prompting them to join the team.

## Invite with a team link

The Starter and Professional plans consist of a single team. On these plans, you can invite others to join the team via a team link.

Anyone who has access to this link will be able to join your team at any time.

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com/).
   - If you’re in a Figma file, click the  Figma menu > **Back to files.**
2. Select  **All projects** from the left sidebar.
3. Click **Share**.
4. Select the **Invite by link** tab.
5. Set [team permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039970673) for those invited via the link.
6. Click **Copy link** and share it with others.

On the Professional plan, users invited via team link are given a free View seat by default. You can [change a user’s seat](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/360039960434) at any time.

### Manage team invitation links

Team admins can turn invite links on and off, or reset them.

Resetting a link will:

- Create a brand-new invite URL
- Immediately void any old links, so they can no longer be used

You can also turn off team invite links altogether to help keep your team secure and help you ensure that only intended members can join.

1. Open the [file browser](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/14381406380183-Guide-to-the-file-browser) by logging in to your account at [figma.com](https://figma.com/).
   - If you’re in a Figma file, click the  Figma menu > **Back to files.**
2. Select  **All projects** from the left sidebar.
3. Click **Share**.
4. Select the **Invite by link** tab.
5. Click  **Team invite link settings**.
   - Click **Reset invite links** to expire the current link and generate a new one.
   - Click **Turn off invite links** to turn off invite links. Members can still invite collaborators by email.

## Troubleshooting

**The invite link option is greyed out.**

Your team admin may have [turned off invitation links](#teaminvitelinks). Ask a team admin to enable them.

**I invited someone, but they can’t see certain files.**

Being in a team doesn’t guarantee access to all projects or files in that team. Check their [file and project permissions](https://help.figma.com/hc/en-us/articles/35361119554711-File-and-project-permissions).
